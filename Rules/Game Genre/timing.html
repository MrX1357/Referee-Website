Rule

  • The game shall consist of 4 quarters of 10 minutes each
  • There should be the interval of play of 2 minutes between the first and second quarter (first half), between the third and fourth quarter (second half) and before each overtime.
  • There shall be a half-time interval of play of 15 minutes
  • In NZ, half time period is often 5 minutes.
  • An interval of play starts:
  • When the game clock signals sounds for the end of the quarter or overtimes
  • An interval of play ends:
  • At the start of the first quarter, after the toss
  • Throw in at the start of the other quarters
  • If the score is tied at the end of the fourth quarter, overtime of 5 minutes shall be played.
  • If the foul is commited near the end of a quarter, the referee shall determine the remaining playing time. A minimum of 0.1s shall be shown on the game clock.
  • If a T, U, or D is commited during an interval of play, any eventual free throw(s) shall be administered before the starting of the following quarters and overtime.
  • The first quarter starts when the ball leaves the hand of the crew chief during the jumpball process
  • All other quarters is at the disposal of the player taking the throw-in
  • The game cannot start if one or both teams are not on the court with 5 Players ready to play.
  • For all games, the first team named in the schedule is the home team.
  • A quarter, overtime or game shall end whent he game clock signals sounds for the end of the quarter or overtime.

    Extra Notes

    Referee shall communicate with scorebench consistently throughout the game to make sure the timing is well managed.

    Referee shall stop the clock at any moment there is a delay of time.
