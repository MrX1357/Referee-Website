Free throws

A free throw is an opportunity to give to a player to score 1 point, uncontested, from a position behind the free-throw line and inside semi-circle.

Free-throw shooter

When a personal, an unsportsmanlike, or a disqualifying contact foul is commited, the free throw(s) shall be awarded as follows.

  • The player on whom the foul was commited shall attempt the free throw(s).
  • If there is a request for him/her to be substituted, he/she must attempt the free throw(s) before leaving the game.
  • If he/she must leave the game due to injury, 5 personal fouls, or having been disqualified, his/her substitute shall attempt the free throw(s). If no substitute is available, any team-mate as designated by his/her head coach shall attempted the free throw(s).

    • When a technical or disqualifying non-contact foul is commited, any member of the opponents' team as designated by his/her head coaches shall attempt the free throw(s).

    The free-throw shooter shall:

  • Take a position behidn the free-throw line and inside the semi-circle
  • Use any method to shoot a free throw in such a way that the ball enters the basketball from above or the ball touching the ring.
  • Release the ball within 5 seconds after it is placed at his/her disposal by the referees
  • Not touch the free-throw line or enter the restricted area until the ball has entered the basket or has touched the ring.
  • Not fake a free throw.

    • Non-free-throw shooters

    The players that are not shooting shall occupy the altenating positions in the above spaces (diagrams)

    During the free throw these players shall not:

  • Occupy free-throw rebound places to which they are not entitled.
  • Enter the restricted area, the neutral zone or leave the free-throw rebound place until the ball has left the hands(s) of the free-throw shooter.
  • Distract the free-throw shooter by their actions

    • The players not in the free-throw rebound place shall remain behind the free-throw line and behind the 3-point goal line until the free throw ends.

    During a free throw(s) followed by another set(s) of free throws or by a throw in (non line-up), all player shall remain behind the free-throw line extended and behind the 3-point goal line.

    Free-throw unsuccessful and the violation is commited by the free-throw shooter

  • Point shall not count
  • Opoonent team throw-in from the free-throw line extended, unless there is a further free throw(s) or possession penalty to be administered.

    • Free-throw successful and the violation is commited by the non-free-throw shooter

  • The point shall count
  • The violation is disregarded.
  • Opponent team throw-in from any place behind that team's endline.

    • Free-throw unsuccessful and the violation is commited by:

  • A free-throw shooting or his/her team-mate on the last free throw, the ball shall be awarded to the opponents for a throw-in from the free-throw line extended unless that team is entitled to further possession.
  • An opponent of the free-throw shooter, a substitute free throw shall be awarded to the free-throw shooter (Another shoot).
  • Both teams, on the last free throw, a jump ball situation occurs.

    • Diagram

    Extra Notes

    If the free-throw shooter commited a violation but the basketball is clearly in, the referee shall determine where he/she uses this advantage to create an advantage. If no advantages are gained, the violation shall be disregarded.
